Penguin Random House

Case Study: Adapting To A Changing America

February 1, 2026 Kathiana Thomas

While Penguin Random House still maintains its status as one of the Big Five publishing houses, after the pandemic its position in the market has suffered from periods of important declines in profit and growth. Most recently, during the first half of 2025, according to Publishers Weekly, PHR profit dropped 12%. The article explained that macroeconomic factors like rising production costs, the political uncertainty, and the restrictive tariff imposed by the Trump administration played a significant role in this fall. The parent company Bertelsmann's spokesman explained that despite the revenue rising to €2.3 billion, operating EBITDA fell 12%, to €255 million ($297.5 million), primarily due “to lower revenues and growth-related expenses in the core U.S. business.”
